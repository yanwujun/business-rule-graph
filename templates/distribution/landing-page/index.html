FAQ (short)

How is Roam different from CodeRabbit / Greptile / Qodo?

Three things: (1) deterministic structural depth via PageRank + Tarjan SCC + Louvain coupling — they ship lighter graph reasoning. (2) 100% local CLI — your code never leaves your machine. (3) EU AI Act Article 12 audit trail built in. CodeRabbit's August 2025 RCE shipped write access to ~1M repos; we don't pull your code into our cloud.

Why does pricing start at $25/dev/mo when CodeRabbit is $24?

Category floor for Team tier (Greptile $30, Snyk $25, Codacy $18). We sit one tier above the floor because the analysis depth is measurably higher. We'll publish the head-to-head precision benchmark on AI-shaped diffs alongside Marketplace launch.

What does "EU AI Act Article 12 ready" mean?

Article 12 takes effect August 2, 2026 for high-risk AI systems and requires automatic event logging built into the system. Penalties: €15M or 3% of global turnover. Roam emits in-toto v1 attestations + chained audit-trail JSONL on every PR-analyze run. Self-Hosted ships an Article-12-by-design conformity pack. Run roam article-12-check on your repo for a 1-page readiness assessment.

Will the CLI stay free?

Yes. Forever. Apache 2.0. The CLI is the top of every funnel and the trust foundation. We monetize the hosted layers (Cloud, Review, Audit Trail) and the enterprise distribution (Self-Hosted), not the local engine.
